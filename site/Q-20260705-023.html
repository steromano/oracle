Hypotheses (causal-driver map)

  • YES: a severe cyclical recession (financial shock, policy error) peaking ~end-2027; structural AI/automation displacement; stagflation.
  • NO: current 4.2% with benign consensus; a point-in-time 10% at end-2027 needs +5.8pp (exceeding the 2009 peak); U-3 is sticky (participation, policy responses cap the headline). Crux: how likely is a severe recession or structural shock pushing U-3 to double digits within 18 months, given a benign starting point?

Latest known state (2026-07)

  • [A] June 2026 U-3 = 4.2% (SA), cooling (payrolls +57k, participation 61.5%). Source: BLS Employment Situation, CNBC.
  • [A] Consensus is benign: FOMC median ~4.3% for 2027; Sahm-rule indicator 0.10 (recession threshold 0.50); NY Fed yield-curve 12-mo recession risk ~15%; market-implied "no US recession by end-2026" ~87.5%. Sources: Federal Reserve SEP (Mar 2026), FRED SAHMREALTIME, Polymarket, Conference Board.
  • [B] Even bearish paths are far from 10%: Deloitte/RSQE-type bear cases see unemployment ~6.5% by 2028 (not 10% by 2027). Sources: Deloitte, U-Michigan RSQE.

Outside view (base rate)

  • US U-3 has reached >=10% at a point-in-time only ~2-3 times in 75 years (1982 ~10.8%, 2009 ~10.0%, 2020 spike ~14.7% but not at a year-end), always after a severe recession. From 4.2%, a specific-date 10% at end-2027 is a deep tail. Anchor ~0.03.
