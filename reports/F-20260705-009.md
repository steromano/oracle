## Hypotheses (causal-driver map, pre-research)
1. **Extreme base difficulty** (NO, very high) — 6 problems open 26 years; 0 of them solved (Poincaré aside).
2. **Claimed-proof-then-accepted** (YES pathway, low) — a claim appears AND the community accepts it within the window; historically claims (Riemann, Navier-Stokes) almost never hold up quickly.
3. **AI-assisted breakthrough** (YES driver, low-but-rising) — formalization + LLM proving is accelerating fast.
4. **Verification/acceptance lag** (NO, high) — even a correct proof usually takes 1-2+ years to gain consensus.
5. **Most-tractable candidate** — Yang-Mills / Navier-Stokes are seen as likelier than Riemann / P-vs-NP if one falls.

Crux flagged for research: is there a *credible current* assault on one of the six, especially AI-assisted?

## Latest known state (2026)
- **[A]** Only the Poincaré Conjecture has ever been resolved (Perelman; prize 2010). No AI system has solved any Millennium problem. Sources: Wikipedia (Millennium Prize Problems), Clay Institute.
- **[B/C]** Claims circulating in 2026 (someone touting a forthcoming Lean proof; a document claiming to resolve all seven) — unverified, treat as crank until the community accepts them. They do NOT meet our acceptance bar. Source: Manifold/news, arXiv preprints.
- **[A] Real AI accelerant:** in 2026, GPT-5.2 + the Aristotle/Lean pipeline produced accepted proofs of several long-open **Erdős problems**, verified by Terence Tao. Sources: 36kr, Medium (Cogni), arXiv:2510.01346 (Aristotle). This is genuine progress on hard research problems — but Erdős problems are a different (more self-contained) tier than the Millennium problems.
- **[B]** Expert view: the 2027/2030 horizons for a Millennium solution are considered "almost impossible" (partly the 2-year Clay-award rule, which our spec does NOT require, but community acceptance is also slow). Source: forecasting/expert commentary.

## Why low, but above the naive 0.01
The base rate is ~0 and the acceptance bar is high, so the naive 0.01 is defensible. But the 2026 Erdős-problem results show AI can now genuinely close previously-open research problems with expert verification — a novel accelerant the pure base rate ignores. It lifts the tail (a tractable candidate like Navier-Stokes/Yang-Mills, aided by formalization, is the plausible YES path), without making acceptance-by-2027 likely.
