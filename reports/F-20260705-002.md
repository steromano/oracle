# Evidence & reasoning — F-20260705-002 (US govt ≥10% stake in a frontier AI lab by end-2026)

## Hypotheses (causal-driver map, pre-research)
1. **Admin equity appetite** (YES, med) — 2025 precedent of taking equity in strategic firms.
2. **No funding/conversion vehicle for labs** (NO, high) — a ≥10% stake needs a huge fresh outlay.
3. **Lab structure/willingness** (NO, med-high) — PBCs, Musk, IPO plans; would they cede ≥10%?
4. **National-security framing** (YES, med) — "strategic asset" logic could drive a large stake.
5. **Short horizon + execution** (NO, med) — ~6 months to decide *and legally close* a novel deal.
6. **Threshold-dodging substitution** (NO, med) — the plausible move is a form *below* 10% (or golden shares, which our spec excludes).

Cruxes: appetite/security (#1/#4) vs no-mechanism/threshold-dodging (#2/#6).

## Latest known state (as of 2026-07-05) — the situation is LIVE
- **[A] 2026-07-02** — OpenAI proposed giving the US government a **5% stake** (~$42.6B on its $852B March-2026 valuation), via an Alaska-Permanent-Fund-style sovereign vehicle; framework floats asking other labs for similar ~5%. Sources: cnbc.com, ft/notus.org, intelligentgov.tech.
- **[A] 2026-06-05** — Trump confirmed the White House is *examining* stakes and expects to meet "all the big ones"; whether the admin will *pursue* is unclear. Source: notus.org, cnbc.com.
- **[B]** Reporting of a deliberate **"9.9% model"** — i.e. structured *just under* 10%. Source: tech-insider.org.
- **[A]** Anthropic publicly states it is **not** in these discussions. Source: cryptobriefing.com.

## Precedent (outside view)
- **[A]** Intel: US took a **10%** stake (Aug 2025) — but funded by *converting existing CHIPS grants* (~$8.9B), a cheap mechanism absent for AI labs. MP Materials: DoD ~15% (July 2025). Sources: cnn.com, csis.org, intc.com.
- For a frontier lab there is no grant to convert: **10% of OpenAI ≈ $85B fresh outlay.** This is precisely why the live proposals are 5% / 9.9%.

## Why the ≥10% threshold specifically is unlikely (crux resolved)
Research revises the base rate *up* for "some federal stake in a frontier lab" (there is a live, named proposal + presidential interest), but the **≥10%** bar is a different object: (a) the actual proposals cluster **below** 10% (5%, "9.9% model"), (b) ≥10% of a leading lab is an ~$85B fresh commitment with no conversion vehicle, (c) it must be **legally closed** by 2026-12-31, and (d) golden shares are excluded by the spec. A ≥10% stake in a *cheaper smaller* frontier lab (Mistral/SSI/Thinking Machines) is a live tail but politically less likely than the leading-lab focus.
